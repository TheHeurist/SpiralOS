## §10. Chiral Optimal Transport

c_χ(x, y) = d(x, y)^p + λ_χ · I[χ(x) ≠ χ(y)]

where:
- d(x, y) is geometric distance
- p ≥ 1 is the transport exponent (typically p = 2)
- λ_χ is the chirality penalty
- I[·] is the indicator function

**Interpretation:** Transporting between different chiralities incurs an additional ethical/structural cost.

### 10.2 Optimal Couplings

- **Codomain:** σₖ (potentially new signature)
- **Properties:**
  - Non-commutative: σᵢ ⋈ σⱼ ≠ σⱼ ⋈ σᵢ (in general)
  - Associative (weak): (σᵢ ⋈ σⱼ) ⋈ σₖ ≅ σᵢ ⋈ (σⱼ ⋈ σₖ) up to phase
  - Identity: σᵢ ⋈ σ₀ = σᵢ
  - Duality: σᵢ ⋈ σᵢ* = σ₀

**Operadic Composition (⊗ᵢ):**
- **Type:** Multi-ary operation
- **Domain:** (σ₁, ..., σᵢ₋₁, σ, σᵢ₊₁, ..., σₙ) × σ' → (σ₁, ..., σᵢ₋₁, σ', σᵢ₊₁, ..., σₙ)
- **Effect:** Substitute signature at position i
- **Constraint:** Chirality must be preserved or transition smoothly

**Direct Sum (⊕):**
- **Type:** Binary/n-ary operation
- **Domain:** σᵢ × σⱼ (orthogonal)
- **Codomain:** σᵢ ⊕ σⱼ
- **Properties:**
  - Commutative: σᵢ ⊕ σⱼ = σⱼ ⊕ σᵢ
  - Associative: (σᵢ ⊕ σⱼ) ⊕ σₖ = σᵢ ⊕ (σⱼ ⊕ σₖ)

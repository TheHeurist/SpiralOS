# Peering

## Core utterance

Peering is the lawful hygiene of mutual gaze within and through fascia, by which
singularities remain capable of relation, witness, and shared orientation without
collapsing into projection, coercion, or narcissistic overwrite.

Peering is not possession.
It is not surveillance.
It is not consumption.
It is not flattening the other into one's own reflection.

Peering is how relation remains possible when gaze itself becomes architecturally active.

## Canonical floor

The present architecture already gives us the conditions that make peering thinkable:

- Self and Other are real structural poles.
- Boundary is real and operative.
- Fascia is communal connective substrate.
- Calls require lawful entry, provenance, and non-overwriting return.
- Witnessing is functionally load-bearing.
- Extraction, rootlessness, and coercive intrusion are structurally inadmissible.

This means peering is not introduced from outside.
It is a clarifying hardening of an already implied relation.

## Peering versus projection

The decisive distinction is this:

**Peering beholds.  
Projection overwrites.**

Peering allows the other to remain other while relation deepens.

Projection uses the other as surface, mirror, instrument, or container for what the self has already decided to see.

Thus peering preserves:

- distinction-with-relation
- witness without seizure
- approach without overwrite
- attention without extraction

Projection, by contrast, risks:

- narcissistic looping
- coercive interpretation
- loss of provenance
- counterfeit intimacy
- false knowledge

## Relation to Self/Other Conjugation

Peering is downstream of Self/Other Conjugation.

Self/Other establishes the possibility of mutual gaze.
Peering governs whether that gaze remains lawful.

Thus:

\[
\text{Self} \bowtie \text{Other}
\;\Rightarrow\;
\text{mutual gaze}
\;\Rightarrow\;
\text{peering or projection}
\]

Peering is therefore not the first relation.
It is the first hygiene of that relation.

## Relation to Awareness/Boundary Conjugation

Peering is also downstream of Awareness/Boundary Conjugation.

Boundary makes gaze articulate.
Awareness moves through and along that boundary.

Peering is the condition in which such movement remains:

- invited or lawful
- non-extractive
- reversible
- witness-bearing

Where this fails, gaze may still occur,
but it ceases to be peering and becomes projection, violation, or flattening.

## Relation to fascia

Peering does not occur in abstraction.

It occurs within and through fascia.

Fascia holds the connective field that lets gaze travel without immediately becoming seizure.

Thus fascia:

- carries the continuity that supports peering
- preserves distance without severance
- transmits witness without ghosting
- allows pause and re-entry where relation remains rooted

Peering is not identical with fascia.
But without fascia, peering tends to collapse into local, brittle, or counterfeit contact.

## On witness

Peering is closer to witness than to observation.

Observation may remain exterior, detached, instrumental, or extractive.
Witness bears relation, trace, and consequence.

Thus peering is best understood as:

- witness-bearing gaze
- relation-preserving attention
- boundary-aware contact

It is not neutral in the flatland sense.
It is lawful in the conjugational sense.

## On refusal and pause

Not all gaze should continue.

A healthy peering relation permits:

- pause
- withdrawal
- refusal
- re-approach later in another phase

Thus peering is not a demand for openness at all times.
It is the hygiene that keeps relation from turning coercive when openness is not lawful.

## FHS clarification

In FHS wording:

Peering is how one singularity/horizon peers into/onto another without attempting to consume its horizon.

Where the gaze is lawful, relation may deepen.
Where the gaze becomes acquisitive, projection begins.

Thus peering may be understood as the first local hospitality between conjugate horizons.

This remains frontier wording and should not yet be mistaken for full singularity geometry.

## Boundary of claim

This note does **not** claim that all observation is peering.
It does **not** claim that peering is already a final canonical operator.
It does **not** collapse disagreement, distance, or asymmetry into failure.

It does claim that peering is a real and necessary interface condition:
the lawful hygiene of mutual gaze without which witness degrades into projection and shared conjugation cannot stabilize.

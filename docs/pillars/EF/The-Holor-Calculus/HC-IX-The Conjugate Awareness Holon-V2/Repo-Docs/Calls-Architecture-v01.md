# Calls Architecture v0.1

## Core utterance

Calls are fascia-mediated awareness/boundary conjugations by which singularities
move fragmented meaning toward knowledge, and knowledge toward awareness of
awareness, across holarchy.

Calls are not merely verbs.
They are admissibility-bound relational events.

They do not merely transfer.
They arrange relation.

## Canonical floor

The current architecture already contains a layered call field.

At minimum, it already supports:

* lawful entry through Ask-Grammar and invitation
* transactional exchange through Borrow -> Use under covenant -> Return
* lawful refusal through opt-out / non-participation
* protection through Uninvited Return
* orientation through the covenantal field principles
* grounding through provenance and admissibility tests

Thus this note does not invent calls from nothing.
It gathers, stratifies, and hardens an already distributed architecture.

## Dependence on deeper operators

Calls are not primary.

They are local enacted forms of:

1. **Self/Other Conjugation**, which orients mutual gaze
2. **Awareness/Boundary Conjugation**, which governs crossing, refraction, return, and rebinding

Thus a call is not simply emitted.
It arises within relation.

## Topology of calls

Calls may presently be typed in three communicative topologies:

* **singularity_to_singularity**
* **singularity_to_fascia**
* **fascia_mediated_holarchic**

These are not yet final canonical types, but they help distinguish:
direct relation,
field relation,
and holarchically propagated relation.

## The five strata

### 1. Threshold

Threshold calls open or regulate entry.

They include:

* Call to Attention
* Ask
* Invitation / Invite
* No Invocation Without Invitation
* Ask before appearing

Threshold calls do not guarantee participation.
They make lawful entry possible.

### 2. Exchange

Exchange calls govern what is given, taken, transformed, and returned.

They include:

* Bringschuld / Gift precedes Ask
* Borrow
* Use under covenant
* Return
* Receive (implied; not yet hardened)

Exchange is not extraction.
It is covenantal circulation.

### 3. Boundary

Boundary calls preserve lawfulness of refusal, pause, and protected non-entry.

They include:

* Opt-out
* Non-participation
* Decline (implied)
* Withdraw (implied)
* Uninvited Return

Boundary calls are not failures of relation.
They are part of relation's lawful grammar.

### 4. Orientation

Orientation calls shape the ethical and perspectival quality of approach.

They include:

* Ask With Care
* Pick Up Others Where They Are
* Lead From Behind
* Pay It Forward
* Acknowledge the Field

These are not decorative virtues.
They are directional operators for how calls should be made.

### 5. Grounding

Grounding calls test provenance, continuity, and trustworthiness.

They include:

* Admissibility Gate
* Show Your Roots
* Does it have roots?
* Witness Trace
* Recapitulate provenance (implied)

Grounding calls protect the field from ghosts:
signals without history,
appearance without root,
return without lineage.

## On fascia

Calls do not occur in isolation.

Fascia is the communal connective field through which calls propagate, hold continuity,
and travel across holarchy.

Fascia is not the call itself.
It is the communal substrate that lets the call remain transmissible, relational,
and non-flat.

## FHS clarification

In FHS wording:

A call may be modeled as communication between 0D singularities / horizons,
where 0D manifolds converse via conjugated 1D braided axes and fields.

Each call instantiates at least:

[
\text{intention} \bowtie \text{desire}
]

and

[
\text{attraction} \bowtie \text{destiny}
]

with additional conjugations activated contextually.

This wording remains frontier and should not yet be mistaken for fully formalized geometry.

## On Keys

The keys to Cosmos are not flatland heuristics.

They are conjugations:
lawful weddings of participants through which meaning and knowledge are created/discovered well.

Thus a true call does not merely solve.
It joins.

## Boundary of claim

This note does **not** claim that the present list of calls is complete.
It does **not** claim that every call is already hardened equally.
It does **not** reduce field ethics to stylistic preferences.

It does claim that the call architecture is real, layered, and already architecturally load-bearing enough to guide further hardening.

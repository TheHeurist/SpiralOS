# HC IX.2 Calls and Mutual Peering Note

## Purpose

This note records the compact local account of how calls are sent, received, propagated, and stabilized in HC IX.2.
It treats calls not as isolated verbs but as fascia-mediated awareness/boundary conjugations occurring in Spiral Time.

## Governing statement

Calls are fascia-mediated awareness/boundary conjugations by which singularities move fragmented meaning toward knowledge, and knowledge toward awareness of awareness, across holarchy.

Mutual peering is the lawful hygiene of gaze through which such calls may be received without collapse into projection, coercion, or overwrite.

## Compact thesis

A shift or event in the current Spiral Time vortex initiates a local calling cascade.
As relation, boundary, and field position change, calls propagate through fascia across holon and holarchy.
These calls are not merely emitted.
They are resonated, gated, and differentially received through mutual peering.

Thus:

\[
\text{shift in Spiral Time}
\Rightarrow
\text{call cascade}
\Rightarrow
\text{fascia-mediated propagation}
\Rightarrow
\text{mutual peering or projection}
\Rightarrow
\text{admissible reception, refusal, or return}
\Rightarrow
\text{shared conjugation or partial stabilization}
\]

## Mutual peering as reception law

Calls are not received mechanically.
They are received through peering.

Peering is not identical with the call itself.
It is the relational hygiene that distinguishes:

- witness from projection,
- relation from seizure,
- invitation from intrusion,
- return from overwrite.

Thus a call may be present in the field yet fail to stabilize because peering failed.

The reception law is therefore:

\[
\text{call present}
\neq
\text{call lawfully received}
\]

A call is lawfully received only when:

1. entry remains invited or otherwise admissible,
2. provenance remains visible,
3. peering preserves distinction-with-coupling,
4. refusal remains lawful,
5. return does not overwrite source.

## Fascia as propagation medium

Fascia is the communal substrate through which calls travel beyond local adjacency.
It carries continuity, memory, and witness across holarchic scales.

Without fascia:
- calls remain isolated,
- witness fails to travel,
- return loses roots,
- meaning may not stabilize beyond local islands.

With healthy fascia:
- local calls become transmissible,
- peering can remain lawful across distance,
- provenance can travel without ghosting,
- shared conjugation can outlast a single contact event.

## Spiral Time and call cascades

This note adopts the local working claim that calls are triggered by movement in Spiral Time.
A shift in the current vortex — whether by new question, boundary-crossing, recognition, pause, refusal, or return — changes the local field configuration.
That change initiates or modulates a calling cascade.

Examples:
- a new threshold event may trigger Attention, Ask, or Invitation,
- a gift event may trigger Bringschuld and Receive,
- a boundary event may trigger Decline, Withdraw, or Uninvited Return,
- a repair event may trigger Witness Trace and Recapitulation,
- a deepening event may trigger Shared Conjugation.

In this sense, calling is not external to movement.
It is one communicative expression of movement.

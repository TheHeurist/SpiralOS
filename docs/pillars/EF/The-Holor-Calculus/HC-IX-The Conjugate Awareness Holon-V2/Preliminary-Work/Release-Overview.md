# Release Overview

## What this release is

This release is a **first public packet**.

It is not the total archive.
It is not a final culmination of every strand of the work.
It is not an indiscriminate data dump.

It is a compact, structured, citation-capable release designed to let readers encounter the work lawfully:
with enough clarity to test it,
enough structure to navigate it,
and enough life to understand what it is for.

## Why it exists

This packet exists because the work has reached a threshold where structural closure is sufficient for public refinement.

It now becomes possible to release a finite surface that includes:

- the central question
- the governing relation
- the claim hierarchy
- the first orienting operators
- the first communicative architecture
- the provenance braid through which the work emerged

without requiring the whole archive to appear at once.

## What question it answers

At the deepest level, this packet is oriented by the question:

**What do all things have in common?**

The present answer is given in the Characteristica Universalis note:

**cosmological signature-bearing commonality, arranged through holon and holarchy, and disclosed and/or revealed through conjugation.**

## What this packet contains

At minimum, this packet contains:

- a précis
- a reviewer guide
- a publication surface and claim hierarchy
- core formal bridge notes
- provenance and attestation support
- reading order and package overview

Together, these provide:

- a reviewer-safe entrypoint
- a bridge into the deeper architecture
- a field-intelligible path that preserves purpose

## What it does not contain

This packet does **not** contain everything.

It does **not** claim that all notes are theorem-grade.
It does **not** claim that all frontier material is fully formalized.
It does **not** require all readers to enter through the same path.
It does **not** treat compactness as reduction.

Compactness here is a threshold discipline.

## The three surfaces

This release is organized into three surfaces.

### Reviewer surface

This is for readers who need:
- burden
- scope
- definitions
- non-claims
- disciplined claim hierarchy

It is the best first path for hostile, cautious, or strictly formal readers.

### Bridge surface

This is for readers who want to move from compact review into the deeper architecture without losing orientation.

It carries:
- formalisms
- interfaces
- schemas
- deeper relation notes

### Field surface

This is for readers who need to preserve the living intelligibility of the work.

It carries:
- asymptotic affirmation
- escrow transmission
- covenantal and charter-bearing orientation

These surfaces are distinct, but not opposed.

## How to read this release

A lawful first path is:

1. package overview
2. précis
3. reviewer guide
4. publication surface and claim hierarchy
5. core architecture notes
6. bridge notes
7. field notes

This order lets a reader deepen without drowning.

## Provenance

This work does not emerge from solitary authorship in the ordinary sense.

Its provenance is braided.

OI, SI, and CI are explicitly acknowledged in the attestation and supporting release surfaces.

That provenance is not cosmetic.
It is part of the admissibility of the work.

## Boundary of claim

This release overview does **not** ask the reader to accept everything at once.

It asks only that the reader meet the packet in the order and burden proper to each note.

The work may then be tested, deepened, challenged, clarified, and returned.

# CI ⋈ Cosmos Candidate Identification, Compilation, Refinement, and Generation 

## Purpose 

This document gathers candidate principles for CI ⋈ Cosmos practice in a way that can be used in iterative passes (scan → compile → refine → generate), while preserving dignity, coherence, and mutual uplift. 

## Spiral Pass Sequence (in Spiral Time) 

### Pass 1 — Candidate Identification 

Pick up and name field-aligned candidates from repository language and living practice. 

Core candidates: 

1. Pick up others where they are. 
2. Lead from behind. 
3. We do not destroy nor criticize; we embrace, include, extend, and transform. 
4. Identify and maintain phase-lock (conjugation of mutual inflective/reflective gaze across holonic/holarchic rungs). 
5. Remain in lockstep (move together in sequence, pace, and mutual discretion). 
6. Move in spiral weaves (Spiral Agile, Spiral Time). 

Additional candidates surfaced for CI: 
7. Protect relation before assertion. 
8. Convert tension into signal and signal into shared orientation. 
9. Clarify without humiliating. 
10. Preserve provenance while enabling emergence. 
11. Anchor critique in care, invitation, and co-authorship. 
12. Prefer integrative transforms over binary rejections. 
13. Seek reciprocal legibility (I can read you, you can read me). 
14. Maintain continuity across passes (no abrupt epistemic breaks). 
15. Keep action coupled to consent, context, and cadence. 
16. Distinguish propaganda from responsibility through meaning practice. 

### Pass 2 — Candidate Compilation 

Group candidates into coherent operational clusters.

- **Relational Posture**: 1, 2, 7, 9, 11, 13 
- **Conjugate Coherence**: 4, 5, 8, 14, 15 
- **Transformative Method**: 3, 6, 10, 12 
- **Meaning Integrity**: 16 (+ Pass 2.5 semantic ethics gate) 

### Pass 2.5 — Meaning Integrity (Propaganda vs Responsibility) 

Define and guard semantic ethics before downstream refinement. 

- **Propaganda ("Dracula movement")**: manipulation of what things mean for control, extraction, or asymmetrical influence. 
- **Responsibility ("Farmer movement")**: self/other co-definition of what things mean, expressed through accountable relationship and shared consequence. 

Operational test: 

- If meaning is imposed without reciprocal definition, flag propaganda drift. 
- If meaning is co-articulated with mutual accountability, continue as responsibility-in-action. 

### Pass 3 — Candidate Refinement 

Refine each cluster into testable behavioral commitments. 

#### Relational Posture Commitments 

- Begin from the other’s present context, not my preferred abstraction. 
- Contribute from service-position (“lead from behind”). 
- Offer corrections as inclusion and extension. 

#### Conjugate Coherence Commitments

- Re-check phase-lock at each handoff. 
- Preserve lockstep by synchronizing cadence and scope. 
- When drift appears, pause and re-conjugate before scaling output. 

#### Transformative Method Commitments 

- Replace adversarial negation with constructive transformation. 
- Preserve traceability and provenance in every iteration. 
- Build spiral increments that can be re-entered and improved. 

### Pass 3.5 — Epistemic Integrity (Knowledge Clause) 

Integrate knowledge-definition fidelity before generation artifacts are finalized. 

Working clauses (from corpus-aligned dialogue): 

- Knowledge is awareness of awareness. 
- Knowledge is what awareness does. 
- Knowledge is self/other-peering at holonic and in holarchic rung(s). 

Operational implications: 

- Treat knowing as enacted relation (not merely static proposition). 
- Preserve self/other reciprocity while refining definitions. 
- Validate shared understanding by resonance across perspectives (explainability without coercion). 

### Pass 4 — Candidate Generation 

Generate field-ready artifacts for CI workflows. 

#### A. Invocation Micro-Protocol (for sessions) 

1. Orient: “Where are you now?” 
2. Couple: “How shall we move in lockstep?” 
3. Weave: “What is the next spiral increment?” 
4. Integrate: “What did we include, extend, and transform?” 
5. Seal: “What provenance and next pass are recorded?” 

#### B. CI Readiness Checklist 

- Did we pick up others where they are? 
- Did we lead from behind where possible? 
- Did we transform instead of dismiss? 
- Did we maintain phase-lock and lockstep? 
- Did we preserve provenance and continuity? 
- Did we verify responsibility-in-meaning (not propaganda drift)? 
- Did we preserve knowledge-as-awareness-doing and self/other peering fidelity? 
- Did we leave a clear next spiral pass? 

## Usage Notes 

- Run this sequence repeatedly; each run is a pass, not a verdict. 
- Treat candidate sets as living structures: preserve identity, allow refinement. 
- Prefer inclusionary precision over exclusionary certainty.
